Instagram for iPad

As a regular user of Instagram, I decided to work on an unsolicited redesign of their iOS app that focuses on the support the iPad. The exercice here was to refine the experience and allow the app to take full advantage of the larger display of the device.

Stories

For this part of the app, I thought it would be interesting to explore with story previews. Displaying a grid of stories will allow the user to get a sneak peak of all the stories available. This is especially useful when you follow a large amount of accounts and want to have a better overview of the all content available.

Each story is displayed in fullscreen in order to keep a good immersion. Because of technical constraints (photo size, video compression), stories are limited to a fixed container container.

Feed & Notifications

The notifications list has been moved to a dropdown menu on the right of the navigation bar. This allows the menu to be accessible in most screens of the app. The icon has been updated with a more generic icon that works for a variety of notification types.

Unlike stories, the feed is using a 1 column layout, like on a phone. I experimented with a multi-columns layout but it seemed better suited for browsing/discovering new content. Having 1 column reduces the number of posts displayed in the viewport. It results in a better focus on the posts, as the goal here is not to “consume content” but rather to offer an experience that allows the content to stand out.

Posts have the possibility to have a caption, displayed right under the image and above the post’s actions and comments.

Navigation

Instagram’s current navigation system isn’t perfectly suited for the iPad. It relies a lot on swipe action which are less user friendly on such a large display. While I understand the decision to integrate the stories in the main “Home” view (discoverability, engagement...), I still think it makes a lot of sens to separate them from the traditional feed.
